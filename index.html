Pablo Muñoz Suárez

I am currently a PhD researcher in the IdCONTROL project, part of the MR-DYNAMO Austrian-funded initiative. Since October 2025, I have been working with Barbara Kaltenbacher at the Department of Mathematics of the University of Klagenfurt. I am also a research engineer since December 2023 in the laboratory of Rodrigo Ramos-Jiliberto, affiliated with the GEMA Center. My work is connected to the project Dynamics of ecological networks: ecological traps, maladaptation, and the robustness of metacommunity dynamics, supported by ANID/FONDECYT grant 1231321.

In September 2024, I defended my master's thesis, “Finite element approximation for a convective Brinkman--Forchheimer problem coupled with a heat equation”, at the Universidad Técnica Federico Santa María (UTFSM), under the supervision of Enrique Otarola and Gilberto Campana.

In January 2023, I defended my thesis in Mathematical Civil Engineering at UTFSM, titled “Bifurcation Analysis for Dynamical Models in Excitatory–Inhibitory Neural Networks in the Presence of Electrochemical Synapses,” under the supervision of Pablo Aguirre and Patricio Orio.

In December 2023, I defended my Diploma in Philosophy of Sciences, with a specialization in Complex Systems, at the Institute of Philosophy and Complexity Sciences (IFICC). My thesis, "Modelling of (M, R)-Systems and Autopoiesis I", explored the mathematical and conceptual foundations of Robert Rosen's (M, R)-systems; a theoretical framework for understanding the organization of living systems, and their connections to the theory of autopoiesis developed by the Chileans Maturana and Varela.
